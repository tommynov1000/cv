I love the level of automation brought forth from computers.

The amount of work that can be done from your phone is astounding. I remember when I got my first flip phone and being absolutely floored with the fact that I no longer had to rely on my parents to help me communicate with my friends. This level of fascination led me down a path where I wanted to continue learning more about what I could do with the technology surrounding me. In middle and high school, I built multiple computers just for myself to play with because I thought it was hilarious to spam my friend with emails from my own mail server.

Eventually, my mischievous actions no longer became entertaining enough, so I made things more difficult for myself by building the entire infrastructure of friend's business. I was lucky enough to accomplish this right before I went to college to become a Nanoscientist. It was here that I learned I was not a fan of waiting around in a lab for test results. Although, not all was doom and gloom; as part of my curriculum, I was tasked with creating a programmable temperature control module using Java where I once again fell in love with computers, but from a different aspect: development. It was my first time experiencing the level of automation I could truly create by using algorithms, UI/UX methodologies and scripting. This newfound love led me towards the start of my career in IT.

Now, I am working towards becoming a DevOps engineer, where that same passion towards automation remains within me. I have an ever increasing amount of skills to learn, and yet more to practice. Every day brings new possibilities.
